The history of boba tea

Daniel Jacob

The origins of boba tea go back to T'ai-nan City, in Taiwan. It's beginning can be traced to Chang Fan Shu, an entrepreneur who developed a tea that would be shaken in a martini mixer, creating a foamy surface. It was served cold and called shou yao (or hand-shaken) tea. In 1986, another entrepreneur named Tu Zonghe added fen yuan, or tapioca balls, into green tea and sold it in T'ai-nan. This simple stand sparked a now international craving for bubble tea.
